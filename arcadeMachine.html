Every year, NC State University hosts the First Year Engineering Design Day (FEDD). During this event, first year engineering students come together to present a project they've designed and built over the semester. The public is then invited to view and interact with these projects, testing durability in addition to being judged by a panel of judges.

My team's category was to build an Arcade Machine. Since an arcade machine can mean a variety of devices, we were given various constraints, such as:

  • A $40 budget
  • Needs a mechanism for counting game score (either physical or electronic)
  • Game must be portable
  • Game must allow at least one action involving the skill of the player
  • Game dimensions must be no more than 2' x 3' x 3'
  • Game must be considered safe (no projectiles, explosives, etc.)

While this was a team project, each of our team members concentrated on what we were best at. As such, I did not have any involvement in the programming or wiring of the electronic scoring system, only designing and building the housing.
